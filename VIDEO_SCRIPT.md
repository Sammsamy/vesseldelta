Target: **2 minutes 24 seconds**. Hard cap: **2 minutes 30 seconds**.

Use a real voiceover. Keep the app full screen. Record at `1280 × 720` or another true 16:9 size. Wait for each flow field to pass its checks before recording the result. A clean jump cut may skip the numerical warm-up, but show **UPDATING BOTH MODELS** for one beat so the edit stays honest.

## Copy-paste voice script

> Hi, I’m Fuzlullah Syed, a third-year medical student. VesselDelta lets anyone change a vessel and watch the mechanics respond.

> First, I choose a narrowing. The live model compares it with a straight vessel at the same flow setting. A faster jet forms at the tightest point, and flow changes more sharply near the wall. Results stay hidden until both fields pass their checks.

> Next, a bulge makes flow slow and swirl. Raising the pressure increases wall load but does not speed up flow. Pressure pushes outward. Flow moves along the wall.

> Then I open the wall stress and strength test. This is a separate calculation using a real thin-wall equation: stress equals pressure times radius divided by wall thickness. Its controls use millimeters of mercury, millimeters, and kilopascals. More pressure, a wider vessel, or a thinner wall raises calculated stress.

> The selected 680 kilopascal comparison is only the midpoint of two published tissue group means, not a medical cutoff.

> The four-case section separates what changes now from what changes over time. Shape and pressure run live. Diet, activity, and medicine cards explain longer-term body pathways, then let me choose a pressure what-if. They never invent a personal response.

> When calculated stress crosses that selected value, the wall opens. The tear is a teaching animation triggered by arithmetic, not a simulated rupture or a prediction.

> The outer wall, inner lining, and cut edges create a realistic layered 3D view, but they are shaped from the live 2D boundary. The red blood cells are visual tracers. I built VesselDelta with GPT-5.6 through Codex. It helped implement the D2Q9 boundaries, run tight-lumen tests, verify unit conversion, and narrow medical claims.

> VesselDelta is an educational model, not clinical CFD, medical advice, or a rupture-risk tool.

## Recording rules

- Do not call the tear a simulated rupture.
- Do not call `680 kPa` a failure cutoff. It is the arithmetic midpoint of two ex-vivo group means.
- Do not say that bright color marks damage, plaque, or a future rupture.
- Do not say that the 3D cutaway is a 3D flow solve.
- Keep the persistent threshold warning visible during the tear.
- Do not show private account details in Codex clips.
